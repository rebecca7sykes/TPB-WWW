Choreographic Processes: Structuring movement to convey an idea to an audience. The process usually involves exploring possibilities,
collecting ideas and materials to inform improvisation, recording improvised movement material, choosing and developing movement material,
reworking and structuring movement material, rehearsing and refining choreography, and finally performing choreography.
Choreographic Structures: Forms that help a choreographer develop movement phrases into a complete dance composition, and that are used
as part of the choreographic process.
