Dr. F. Todd Goodson
Associate Professor of Secondary Education, Kansas State University
Dr. Goodson directs the secondary credentialing programs in English, speech/theatre, and journalism at Kansas State University. He teaches an
undergraduate methods course devoted to secondary language arts, supervises students' field experiences, advises undergraduate and graduate
students, and supervises graduate student research. Dr. Goodson also serves as the current Editor of the
Journal of Adolescent and Adult Literacy
, a
publication of the International Reading Association, and he directs the Flint Hills Writing Project, a site of the National Writing Project hosted by
Kansas State University. His areas of interest are Secondary English Education, Adolescent Literacy, and Sociocultural Approaches to Literacy
Pedagogy.
Dr. James Marshall
Professor and Associate Dean, University of Iowa
James Marshall is Professor of English and English Education at The University of Iowa, where he currently serves as Associate Dean for Teacher
Education. Having taught high school English for six years, Dr. Marshall is especially interested in the relationships between writing and literary
understanding and in the ways that literature is discussed in English classrooms. His books include Ways
of Knowing: Research and Practice in the
Teaching of Writing
, with James Davis;
Teaching Literature in the Secondary School
, with Richard Beach; and
The Language of Interpretation:
Patterns of Discourse in Discussions of Literature
, with Peter Smagorinsky and Michael Smith. He is immediate past-chair of National Council of
Teachers of English Standing Committee for Research, a former Trustee of NCTE's Research Foundation, and Chair of the English Language Arts
Assessment for the National Board of Professional Teaching Standards. He has won the James N. Murray Award for Teaching, the Outstanding
Teaching Award, and the Regents' Award for Faculty Excellence, and the Hancher-Finkbine Medallion for Faculty Leadership from The University of
Iowa.
Tiffany Chen Raiser, Ph.D.
Lecturer of Rhetoric and Composition, American Studies Department, Arizona State University West
Dr Raiser's research focuses on textual studies and criticism, colonial and post-colonial discourses, cultural studies, literature in its contexts, and web-
based instruction in the field of English Studies. She teaches writing and literature courses. Dr. Raiser holds a B.A. degree in English Language and
Literature from Fu-Jen University, Taiwan, an M.A. in Literature in Its Contexts from Sussex University, England, and a M.Ed. in Educational
Technology and a Ph.D. in English from Arizona State University.
