Instruction in the Language Arts
High-quality instruction is at the heart of all good language arts programs.
A comprehensive, balanced language arts program in which curriculum and
instruction are differentiated according to assessed needs should be provided to
all students (National Research Council 1998; National Reading Panel 2000).
Characteristics of the instruction component in an effective language arts
program are as follows:
? The curriculum for reading and the language arts in kindergarten through
grade three provides explicit and systematic instruction and diagnostic
support in:
每 Phonemic awareness
每 Phonics
每 Decoding
每 Word-attack skills
每 Spelling
每 Vocabulary
每 Fluency in reading connected text
每 Comprehension skills
每 Writing skills and strategies and their application
每 Listening and speaking skills and strategies
? The curriculum for reading and the language arts in grades four through twelve
provides explicit and systematic instruction and diagnostic support in:
每 Word-attack skills (e.g., decoding and structural as applied to multisyllabic
words)
每 Spelling
每 Vocabulary
每 Fluency in reading connected text
每 Comprehension skills, including contextual skills
每 Text-handling and strategic reading skills
