n the sixth grade students focus on active engagement with the text. They are
required to analyze, identify, define, explain, and critique rather than merely
understand, describe, use, know, and distinguish as they were required to
do in the fifth grade. However, the standards still require students to read aloud
narrative and expository text fluently and accurately and with appropriate pacing,
intonation, and expression.
As in the fifth grade, an instructional priority in the sixth grade is an increased
focus on advanced forms of evaluation in expository critique and literary criticism
and advanced presentations on problems and solutions.
