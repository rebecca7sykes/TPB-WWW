3.0 Literary Response and Analysis
Students read and respond to historically or culturally significant works of lit-
erature that reflect and enhance their studies of history and social science. They
conduct in-depth analyses of recurrent patterns and themes. The selections in
Recommended Literature, Kindergarten Through Grade Twelve
illustrate the quality
and complexity of the materials to be read by students.
Structural Features of Literature
3.1 Articulate the relationship between the expressed purposes and the
characteristics of different forms of dramatic literature (e.g., comedy,
tragedy, drama, dramatic monologue).
3.2 Compare and contrast the presentation of a similar theme or topic
across genres to explain how the selection of genre shapes the theme or
topic.
Narrative Analysis of Grade-Level-Appropriate Text
3.3 Analyze interactions between main and subordinate characters in a
literary text (e.g., internal and external conflicts, motivations, relation-
ships, influences) and explain the way those interactions affect the plot.
3.4 Determine characters' traits by what the characters say about them-
selves in narration, dialogue, dramatic monologue, and soliloquy.
3.5 Compare works that express a universal theme and provide evidence to
support the ideas expressed in each work.
