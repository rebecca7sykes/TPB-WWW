Instructional Delivery
Objective, thorough literary criticism is not easy to achieve. Instruction should
begin with a clear notion of what literary criticism is and how the approach cur-
rently under study (i.e., political) differs from approaches taught in earlier grades
(e.g., historical). A good way to frame the instruction is to have students first
read examples of political literary criticism before going to source selections and
eventually writing a formal critique.
Teachers should provide substantial guidance throughout this sequence, par-
ticularly in the form of explicit instruction in the elements of literary criticism
unique to the political approach.
