California is committed to the fact that the English每language arts content

state to evaluation criteria aligned with the standards and curriculum framework.
These criteria include elements of the 2002 criteria and address current docu-

to produce instructional materials that will help teachers to close the achievement
gap that persists despite gains in students' reading/language arts achievement
since the last adoption cycle of instructional materials. To that end the criteria
emphasize increased vocabulary, oral reading fluency, and writing. There is a
deeper focus on the instructional needs of English learners, students with dis-
abilities, struggling readers, and students who use African American vernacular
English.
The criteria will provide instructional materials in 2008 to support all students'
