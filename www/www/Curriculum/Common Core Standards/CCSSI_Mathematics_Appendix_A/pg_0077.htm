Unit 3: trigonometry of General triangles and trigonometric Functions
Students develop the Laws of Sines and Cosines in order to find missing measures of general (not necessarily right)
triangles. They are able to distinguish whether three given measures (angles or sides) define 0, 1, 2, or infinitely many
triangles. This discussion of general triangles open up the idea of trigonometry applied beyond the right triangle¡ª
that is, at least to obtuse angles. Students build on this idea to develop the notion of radian measure for angles and
extend the domain of the trigonometric functions to all real numbers. They apply this knowledge to model simple
periodic phenomena.
