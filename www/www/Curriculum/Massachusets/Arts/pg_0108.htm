PreK¨C12 STANDARD 9: Inventions, Technologies, and the Arts
Students will describe and analyze how performing and visual artists use and have used materials,
inventions, and technologies in their works.

By the end of
grade 4
Students will
9.1 When using art materials or handling and viewing artifacts or musical
instruments, ask and answer questions such as
? "What is this made of?"
? "How does this instrument produce sound?"
? "Would I design this differently?"
? "Who first thought of making something like this?"
For example, students examine a variety of percussion instruments,
experiment with the different sounds they make, and learn about the cultures
in which they were made.
By the end of
grade 8
Students will
9.2 Identify and describe examples of how the discovery of new inventions and
technologies, or the availability of new materials brought about changes in
the arts in various time periods and cultures
9.3 Identify and describe examples of how artists make innovative uses of
technologies and inventions
For example, students research Matthew Brady's portrait photographs and
Civil War battlefield photographs.
9.4 Identify and describe examples of how contemporary artists use computer
technology in their work
For example, students find out how software is used for animation, graphic
design, theatrical lighting and sound design, choreography, or musical
composition.
By the end of
basic study in
grades 9¨C12
Students will
9.5 Identify and describe examples of how technological development in one
artistic domain can influence work in other artistic domain
9.6 Compare the available materials, inventions, and technologies of two
historical periods or cultures and explain their effect on the arts
9.7 Identify and describe the examples of the persistence of traditional historical
materials and technologies in contemporary artworks
For example, a student interested in textile design investigates the works of
artists who use plants to make dyes and finds out why they choose to use
these traditional techniques.
