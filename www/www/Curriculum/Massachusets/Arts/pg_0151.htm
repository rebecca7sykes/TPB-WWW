Technology Competencies and the Arts
Categories
Competencies by Grade 8
In the Arts
Students should be able to:
? Use technology resources (word processing, e-mail,
online discussions, Web environments) to
communicate ideas, sounds, images.
? Gather and analyze information using
telecommunications.
? Design, develop, publish and disseminate products
(e.g., Web pages, videotapes) using technology
resources that demonstrate and communicate
curriculum concepts.
? Routinely and efficiently use online information
resources to meet needs for collaboration, research,
publications, and communications
? Collaborate with peers, experts, and others to
contribute to a content-related knowledge base by
using technology to compile, synthesize, produce and
disseminate information, models, and other creative
works
For example, students can:
Use e-mail to communicate
with a students working on
a common theatre or
musical composition
project
Create a web page that
contains an exhibition of
student work and
performances
Use graphic design
software to manipulate
images and text
Use dance software to
visualize choreography
Technology
Research Tools
Students should be able to:
? Use content-specific tools (online
encyclopedias/dictionaries, electronic search tools,
search engines) to locate, evaluate, and collect
information from a variety of sources.
? Evaluate the accuracy, relevance, appropriateness,
comprehensiveness, and bias of electronic
information sources concerning real-world problems
? Routinely and efficiently use online information
resources to meet needs for research
? Select and apply technology tools for research
? Collaborate with peers, experts, and others to
contribute to a content-related
knowledge base by using technology to compile,
synthesize, produce and disseminate information,
models, and other creative works
For example, students can:
Look up vocabulary words
in the arts using an online
dictionary
Locate information about
cultural resources in the
community
Use online tools to locate
artists then use e-mail to
interview them
Technology
Problem-Solving
and Decision-
Making Tools
Students should be able to:
? Use technology resources (simulations, charts) for
problem-solving.
? Determine when technology is useful and select the
appropriate tool(s) and technology resources to
address a variety of tasks and problems
? Investigate and apply expert systems, intelligent
agents, and simulations in real-world situations
For example, students can:
Enlarge an image
appearing on the computer
screen when needed
Identify a research
question; search for
information in print and
online; analyze the
information and compose a
presentation to teach others
