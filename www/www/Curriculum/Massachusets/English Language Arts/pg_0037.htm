GENERAL STANDARD 8: Understanding a Text
Students will identify the basic facts and main ideas in a text
and use them as the basis for interpretation.
(For vocabulary and concept development see General Standard 4.)
When we read a text closely, we work carefully to discern the author 's main ideas and the particular
facts and details that support them. Good readers read thoughtfully and purposefully, constantly check-
ing their understanding of the author 's intent and meaning so that their interpretations will be sound.
