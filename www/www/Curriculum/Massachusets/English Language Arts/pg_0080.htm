GENERAL STANDARD 21: Revising
Students will demonstrate improvement in organization, content, paragraph development,
level of detail, style, tone, and word choice (diction) in their compositions after revising them.
A flawless first draft is a rarity, even for the most gifted writer. Writing well requires two processes that
sometimes appear to be in opposition: creating and criticizing. As they expand their imaginative thinking
on paper, students must at the same time learn the patience and discipline required to reshape and
polish their final work. Revising to get thoughts and words just right can be the most difficult part of
writing, and also the most satisfying.
