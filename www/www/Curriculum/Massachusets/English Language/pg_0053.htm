Reading (R)
R.3 Comprehension: Students will read English fluently and identify
facts and evidence in order to interpret and analyze text.
"Good readers read thoughtfully and purposefully, constantly checking
their understanding of the author's intent and meaning so that their
interpretations will be sound."
(
Massachusetts English Language Arts Curriculum Framework,
p. 35)
In moving from the understanding of concrete facts to abstract and
implied ideas in text, comprehension by English Language Learners is
facilitated through classroom discussion and by activating students' prior
knowledge of the content. Students also benefit from awareness of the
purpose for reading, knowledge of text structure, and a flexible reading
rate that enhances understanding.
June 2003 Massachusetts Department of Education
