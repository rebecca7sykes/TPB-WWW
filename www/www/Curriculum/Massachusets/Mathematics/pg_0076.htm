Refers to standards 8.M.1 and 8.M.4
At the end of every second mile of the Boston Marathon, a typical marathon runner takes
a four ounce cup of water. Instead of drinking all of the water, the runner sips some of it
and then throws the rest on his or her head or body to cool off.
1. Assuming the typical runner drinks half of the water in the cup, how many ounces of
water would an average runner drink during an entire 26.2 mile marathon? Explain
how you found your answer.
2. Suppose that all of the runners in the Boston Marathon behaved like the "typical"
marathon runner described above. About how many gallons of water would the 40,700
runners in the 1996 Boston Marathon have used? Record each step that you used to
find your answer.
