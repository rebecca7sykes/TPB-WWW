Standard 3: Responding to and Analyzing Works of Art
Performance Indicator 3d
Use appropriate terms to reflect a working knowledge of social-musical functions and uses
(appropriate choices of music for common ceremonies and other events).
Students analyze the importance and use of music in their lives
and the lives of others.
(Band, Chorus, Orchestra, General Music)
? Students learn several patriotic songs. These may include "America,"
"America, the Beautiful," "The Star Spangled Banner," "You're a
Grand Old Flag."
? Students discuss the historical significance of these musical
selections, the history of the American flag, and uses of patriotic
music in our culture.
? Teacher introduces a ceremonial song from another culture. Students
may listen, sing, perform a rhythmic pattern or ostinato related to the
music.
? Through class discussion and guided questioning, students determine
the cultural significance of the piece.
? Activity may be extended by discussing similarities and differences in
the social function of the music or by interviewing an adult to find out
how music has impacted his/her life.
Suggested Assessment:
?
Students write a paragraph to describe the importance of music in a
particular ceremony or event (e.g., Bar/Bat Mitzvah, wedding, parade)
they have attended. If possible, an audio example should be
included.
?
As DJ for a patriotic assembly, student chooses three musical
selections for the program and explains reasons for the selections.
