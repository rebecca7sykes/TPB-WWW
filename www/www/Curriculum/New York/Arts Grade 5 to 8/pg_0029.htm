Use traditional or nontraditional sound sources, including electronic ones, in composing and per-
forming simple pieces.
Students use nontraditional sound sources to create sound effects
accompanying a story they have written.
(General Music)
? Students write a short story associated with a holiday or other event
(e.g., a spooky story for Halloween). Special care should be taken to
include sound effects. This activity is done in small groups.
? Students explore nontraditional, or "found sounds," sources, and
choose sounds to fit each sound effect event. The group decides who
is responsible for each sound effect, and chooses a narrator or
narrators.
? Student groups rehearse the story, with sound effects.
? Student groups perform and record (audio or video) their story. (Refer
to Standard 2c.)
? If available, students should input their story into a word processor,
print it and hand it in to the teacher.
? In the written copy, students highlight the words on which sound
effects will occur.
Suggested Assessment:
?

of the sounds in enhancing the story.
