Standard 4: Understanding the Cultural Dimensions and
Contributions of the Arts
Performance Indicator 4d
In performing ensembles, read and perform repertoire in a culturally authentic manner.
Students compare and perform articulations in "classical" and jazz
styles.
(Band, Chorus, Orchestra, General Music)
? Teacher selects a swing-style jazz selection appropriate to the
performing ensemble such as "Satin Doll" (Ellington/Edmondson).
? Teacher demonstrates stylistic differences between straight-ahead
and swing feel.
? Teacher performs a swing rhythm pattern and students echo.
Students transfer their understanding of the concept to the literature.
? Teacher prepares a list of jazz articulations from jazz literature being
studied that represents swing, rock, Latin, and ballad styles.
? Teacher performs articulations in "classical" style and in jazz style
from the literature currently being studied.
? Teacher encourages students to use any recorded accompaniment to
practice the articulations.
Suggested Assessment:
?
Students demonstrate success in comparing and performing
articulations by:
每 Discussing the difference in class.
每 Recognizing the differences, aurally and in print music.
每 Performing the articulation in print music.
每 Writing articulations in their music.
Note: Also addresses Standards 3a, 3b, and 4b.
