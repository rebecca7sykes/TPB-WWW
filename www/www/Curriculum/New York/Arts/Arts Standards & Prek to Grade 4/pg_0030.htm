Students compose a short melody to sing or play on an instrument.
(Band, Orchestra, General Music)
? Suggested guidelines include length (e.g., four measures), meter
(e.g., 4/4 time signature), rhythms (e.g., quarter notes, eighth notes,
eighth rests), pitch sets (e.g., C pentatonic scale), and/or the starting
and ending notes.
? Teacher composes the melodic rhythms, including the correct number
of beats and rhythms.
? Using the composed rhythm, students improvise on their instrument
over the selected pitches until they arrive at a satisfactory melody.
? Students add pitch names to the rhythm notation to complete the
melody.
? Students write composition in standard notation on staff paper.
? Technology: Students use notation software such as Finale Notepad
to print the melody (optional).
? For students with special needs, other options can be explored for
notation such as graphic representations.
