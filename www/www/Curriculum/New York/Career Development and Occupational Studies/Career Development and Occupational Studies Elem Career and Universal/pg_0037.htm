ELEMENTARY SAMPLE ACTIVITY
Essential Question(s):
What do I need to discover about myself that will allow me to handle change in the
workplace?
Title of Activity:
Go with the Flow
K 1 2 3 4 Estimated Time:
30 minutes
OBJECTIVE(S)
Students will identify things they can change and things they cannot
change.
DESCRIPTION OF ACTIVITY
? Teacher reads a fable about change to the class. Students discuss
the main character 's actions and the consequences of those actions
(e.g., how they handled the changing situation).
? Students name things they can change (e.g., our own behavior,
what we say, how we treat others, how we do our assignments).
? Teacher lists the students' ideas on chart paper.
? On a second list, ask students to name things they cannot change
(e.g., the behavior of other people, what other people say, the
weather, physical characteristics).
MATERIALS/RESOURCES
? Chart paper
? Markers
COMMENTS/MODIFICATIONS
? Look at a series of books that focus on change as a central theme.
Examples include:
The Berenstein Bears
(K)
The Polk Street Kids
(upper 2nd - 4th)
Molly's Pilgrim
(3rd - 4th grade)
ASSESSMENT/EVALUATION
? Students have identified behaviors that can be changed and
some that cannot be changed.
