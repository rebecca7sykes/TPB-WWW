COMMENCEMENT SAMPLE ACTIVITY
Content Area(s):
English Language Arts, Global History
Title of Activity:
Who Has Human Rights?
9
10 11 12
Estimated Time:
1 week
OBJECTIVE(S)
Students will apply the ideals of the Helsinki Agreement to the news,
a novel (
In the Time of the Butterflies)
, and themselves.
DESCRIPTION OF ACTIVITY
? Students read
In the Time of the Butterflies
.
? Students read the handout about the Helsinki Agreement and put it
in their own words, using specific examples to explain the components
of the agreement. Students meet in groups to share their
interpretations, and they come to agreement on a group
interpretation. The class discusses these interpretations to ensure
that everyone has a valid understanding.
? Students collect three newspaper articles that identify issues put
forth by the Helsinki Agreement and explain why they think that
article addresses the points in the Helsinki Agreement.
? Students identify five times when basic human rights were
unprotected in the novel
In the Time of the Butterflies
.
? Students work in teams to discuss their findings. Each team
chooses one issue to share with the class. The class discusses each
group's findings.
? Students complete the following writing assignment: "Take the role
of one of the Mirabal sisters from
In the Time of the Butterflies
.
Using that point of view
,
write an editorial that relates your '
situation in terms of human rights being upheld or violated."
MATERIALS/RESOURCES
? Copies of
In the Time of the Butterflies
? Copies of student assignment
? Helsinki Agreement
The Helsinki Agreement states that the freedoms of speech, religion, and
the press are basic human rights. The 35 nations that signed this
agreement in 1975 also agreed to protect the rights of their citizens to get a
fair trial, to earn a living, and to live in safety from attack.
