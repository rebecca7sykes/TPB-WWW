Essential Question(s):

Title of Activity:
Role-Play¡ªEthics
5 6 7 8
Estimated Time:
1 class period
OBJECTIVE(S)
Students will understand how attitudes and choices affect success at
school and at the workplace.
DESCRIPTION OF ACTIVITY
? Students role-play various situations that involve ethical decisions
or actions.
?
Cheating on a test
?
Taking credit for another 's work
?
Gossiping about fellow students
?
Forging a parent's signature
?
Staying home sick when you're not
?
Making fun of a classmate who is different
?
Sneaking "freebies" to families and friends
? Students discuss the ethics involved in each of the situations and
possible results of those actions.
? Students identify similar actions in the workplace and discuss how
those actions could impact one's success on the job.
? Students write a one-page summary of their reactions to the
role-play situations and discussions.
MATERIALS/RESOURCES
? Copies of role-play situations
COMMENTS/MODIFICATIONS
? Teachers or students can create their own ethical situations tailored
to their grade level.
? Summaries could be completed on a computer.
ASSESSMENT/EVALUATION
? Students are evaluated on basis of participation in class discussions.
? Students are evaluated on basis of one-page reaction.
