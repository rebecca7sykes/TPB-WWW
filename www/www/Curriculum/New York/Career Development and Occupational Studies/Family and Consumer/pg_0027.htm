5. Technology is the process and product of human
skill and ingenuity in designing and creating things
from available resources to satisfy personal and
societal needs and wants.
Students:
? demonstrate an awareness of the different types of
technology available to them and of how technology
affects society.
This is evident, for example, when students:
select the appropriate technology for designing and creating a
flyer for a school-sponsored event
identify examples of technology found at home, at school, and in
a business environment
choose a career area and research how technology has changed
that cluster of occupations.
6. Information management focuses on the ability to
access and use information obtained from other
people, community resources, and computer networks.
Students:
? describe the need for data and obtain data to make
decisions.
This is evident, for example, when students:
explain the practical uses of weather forecasting data as it
relates to the farm industry
plan a school store and determine what items might sell best
listen to a presentation about a career area and write a report
summarizing the information.
