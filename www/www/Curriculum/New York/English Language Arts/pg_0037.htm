Standard 3:
Students will read, write, listen, and speak for
critical analysis and evaluation.
? Evaluate the content by identifying
- the author's purpose
- whether events, actions, characters, and/or settings
are realistic
- important and unimportant details
- statements of fact, opinion, and exaggeration, with
assistance
- recurring themes across works in print and media
? Compare and contrast characters, plot, and setting in
literary works
? Analyze ideas and information on the basis of prior
knowledge and personal experience
? Recognize how language and illustrations are used to
persuade in printed and filmed advertisements and in
texts, such as letters to the editor
? Judge accuracy of content to gather facts, with assis-
tance from teachers and parents/caregivers
? Use opinions and reactions of teachers and classmates
to evaluate personal interpretation of ideas, information,
and experience
? Evaluate information, ideas, opinions, and themes in
texts by identifying, with assistance,
-a central idea and supporting details
- missing or unclear information
