Basis for
Theatre Arts
Theatre, the imagined and enacted world of human beings, is one of the primary
ways children at an early age learn about life - about actions and consequences,
about customs and beliefs, about others and themselves. They learn through
their social pretend play and from hours of viewing television and film.
Children use pretend play as a means of making sense of the world; they create
situations to play and assume roles; they interact with peers and arrange
environments to bring their stories to life; they direct one another to bring order
to their drama, and they respond to one another's dramas. In other words,
children arrive at school with rudimentary skills as playwrights, actors,
designers, directors, and audience members; theatre arts education should build
on this foundation. The theatre arts program in the early years starts with and
has a strong emphasis on improvisation, which is the basis of social pretend
play.
