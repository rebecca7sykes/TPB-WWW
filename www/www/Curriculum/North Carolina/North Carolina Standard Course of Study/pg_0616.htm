Biomechanics:
the study of the structure and f unction of biological systems using the methods
of mechanics. The study of biomechanics r anges from the inner workings of a cell to the
movement and development of limbs, the vasculature, and bones. An understanding of the
physiological behavior of living tissues would allow researchers to advance the field of tissue
engineering, as well as develop improved treatments for a wide array of pathologies.
Body Management:
basic skills taught in early years focusing on abilities to control the
body/body parts in actions such as those involving traveling, balancing, rolling, or supporting
body weight.
