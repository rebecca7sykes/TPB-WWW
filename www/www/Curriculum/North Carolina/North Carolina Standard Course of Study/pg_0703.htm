Mathematics
Algebra
Students in the intermediate grades continue to identify and describe
patterns in many situations. Tools, such as calculators and computers, are
used to investigate and discover patterns. Patterns are used in geometry
and other mathematics to develop new concepts. Tables and graphs are
made to show relationships and then students verbally describe the
patterns. Patterns are used to extend student data, suggest rules for
relationships, and make predictions. Students begin to use symbols to
represent unknown quantities. They use the symbols in expressions and
open sentences when describing relationships and solving problems.
Students begin to identify, describe, and analyze situations with constant
or varying rates of change, and compare them.
