Grade 5 Curriculum
Science ¨C Grade 5
Fifth grade students focus on evidence, models, and scientific explanations.
Evidence consists of observations and data on which to base scientific
explanations. Using evidence to understand interactions allows students to predict
changes in natural and designed systems. Models are tentative schemes or
structures that represent real objects. Models help students understand how things
work. Explanations incorporate prior scientific knowledge and new evidence from
observations, experiments, or models into consistent, logical statements. As
students understand more science concepts and processes, their explanations
should become more accurate and logical. Guide student learning to continue to
emphasize the unifying concepts previously introduced as well as the introduction
at grade five of models. The strands provide a context for teaching the content
throughout all goals.
