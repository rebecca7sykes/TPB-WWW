The AP
?
Biology course is equivalent to a two-semester introductory college biology
course. This course covers in greater scope and scale the concepts, knowledge, and skills
introduced in a first level high school biology program. Greater amounts of time and
effort are expected on the part of the student.
The major themes for this course are expected to permeate the entire course. The purpose
of these themes is to tie the curriculum together and assist students in assimilating the
materials into an expandable understanding. The eight major themes are:
1. Science as a Process
2. Evolution
3. Energy Transfer
4. Continuity and Change
5. Relationship of Structure to Function
6. Regulation
7. Interdependence in Nature
8. Science, Technology, and Society
