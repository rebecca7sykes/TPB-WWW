Foreign Language
Requirements
In 2000 the University of North Carolina instituted a two-year foreign
language admissions requirement beginning in the fall of 2004. As a
result the State Board of Education adopted a two-year foreign
language requirement for all students graduating in 2004 in the
college/university preparation course of study. The Spanish for Native
Speakers courses will give heritage language speakers the opportunity
to meet this two-year graduation requirement.
Spanish for Native
Speakers Course
Sequence
Heritage language speakers can enroll in Spanish for Native Speakers
I and Spanish for Native Speakers II. Upon completion of these two
courses, students can proceed to the Advanced Placement (AP)
Spanish where they will be joined by fourth- and/or fifth-year students
who are studying Spanish as a foreign language.
