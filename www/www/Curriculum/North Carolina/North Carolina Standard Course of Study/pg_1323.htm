This course is designed to provide an orientation to the world of work.
Experiences are designed to introduce students to the technical nature of
today's world and the role of productive workers. Activities enable
students to increase self-awareness
and make wise educational and
occupational decisions as they plan for careers. Work-based learning
strategies appropriate for this course include job shadowing and field
trips. Opportunities for leadership development and further application
of instructional competencies are provided through Career Exploration
Clubs of North Carolina (CECNC). The formal career development
planning process often begins within this course.

change careers. Competencies for this course are based on the National
Career Development Guidelines. Strategies for this course include
teamwork, technology, problem-solving, decision-making, goal-
setting, and self-management.
