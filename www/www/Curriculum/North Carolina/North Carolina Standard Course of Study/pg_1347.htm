This course is designed for students interested in sports, entertainment,
and event marketing. Emphasis is placed on the following principles as
they apply to the industry: branding, licensing, and naming rights;
business foundations; concessions and on-site merchandising; economic
foundations; promotion; safety and security; and human relations. Skills
in communications, human relations, psychology, and mathematics are
reinforced in this course. Work-based learning strategies appropriate for
this course include cooperative education, paid/unpaid internships, or
school-based enterprises. Marketing simulations, projects, teamwork,
DECA leadership activities, meetings, conferences, and competitions
provide many opportunities for application of instructional competen-
cies.

sports, entertainment, and event marketing. Emphasis is placed on the
following principles as they apply to the industry: Business manage-
ment, career development options, client relations, ethics, events man-

Sports and Entertainment Marketing I
This fast-paced course challenges students by combining into one course
the content taught in the Marketing and Marketing Management
courses. The curriculum, activities, and resources utilized in this course
are written at the freshman college level. Topics include economics,
marketing research and decision making, domestic and international
markets and influences, human resource development, ethics,
management, and financial analysis. Skills in mathematics, research,
and critical thinking are reinforced in this course. Work-based learning
strategies appropriate for this course include cooperative education and
paid/unpaid internships. Marketing simulations, projects, teamwork,
DECA leadership activities, meetings, conferences, and competitions
provide many opportunities for application of instructional competen-
cies.
