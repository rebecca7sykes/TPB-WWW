Academic Standards for Geography
22 Pa. Code, Chapter 4, Appendix C (#006-275) Final Form-Annex A July 18, 2002 Page # 22
Thematic map:
A geographic representation of a specific spatial distribution, theme or topic (e.g., population density, cattle
production, climates of the world).
Time zone:
A division of Earth, usually 15 degrees longitude, within which the time at the central meridian of the division
represents the whole division.
Topography:
The shape of Earth's surface.
Water cycle:
The continuous circulation of water from the oceans, through the air, to the land and back to the sea. Water
evaporates from oceans, lakes, rivers and the land surfaces and transpires from vegetation. It condenses into
clouds in the atmosphere that may result in precipitation returning water to the land. Water then seeps into
the soil or flows out to sea completing the circulation. Also known as Hydrologic Cycle.
